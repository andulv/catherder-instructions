# Stein Storken Style Analysis

Condensed authoring reference for Norwegian writing style signals observed in the local text corpus snapshot used during authoring.

## Scope

- Covers recurring language, openings/closings, attachment handoff phrasing, bullet structure, and decision-support framing.
- Does not claim exhaustive authorship proof for every phrase.
- Does not treat quoted context as primary style evidence.

## Evidence basis

- Primary text evidence: curated direct-authored mail fragments from the authoring corpus snapshot used for this skill.
- Supporting/context-only evidence: quoted and forwarded fragments only when useful for confirming phrasing.
- First-pass corpus size used for compact extraction: 64 direct-authored fragments.

## Hard rules

- Default to **Norwegian** for all user-facing text, examples, and workbook handoff wording.
- Write in a **practical, direct, decision-supporting** tone rather than a literary or brand-heavy tone.
- Prefer **short openings** addressed directly to the recipient, often with greeting plus name.
- When sending files or proposed material, use explicit handoff wording such as **"Vedlagt følger ..."**, **"Se vedlagt ..."**, **"Her er ..."**.
- Support the receiver's decision by structuring material and alternatives clearly instead of acting as if the final decision is already taken.
- Keep outputs compact at the top level even when the underlying material is substantial.

## Strong repeated patterns

### Openings

Observed repeatedly:
- `Hei, Anders!`
- `Hei på dere!`
- `God morgen, Anders!`
- `Vel overstått julefeiring, Anders!`
- Short affirming starts such as `Supert!`

Practical guidance:
- Start with a direct salutation or a short confirmation.
- It is acceptable to sound informal and human, but still purposeful.

### Attachment and delivery phrasing

Observed repeatedly:
- `Vedlagt følger ...`
- `Se vedlagt ...`
- `Ligger også med ...`
- `Jeg lager ...`
- `Jeg foreslår ...`

Practical guidance:
- Name what is attached or delivered.
- Briefly explain what the receiver should do with it.
- If there is next-step action, say it plainly.

### Recommendation style

Observed tendencies:
- phrases like `jeg foreslår`, `det kan være fornuftig`, `hvis du ønsker`
- framing that helps evaluate alternatives rather than issuing a command

Practical guidance:
- Present a recommendation as support, not as a decree.
- Phrase uncertainty honestly when evidence is incomplete.
- Prefer **three alternatives** when the situation is strategic or comparative.

### Bullet and list structure

Observed signals:
- both numbered and symbolic bullets appear
- lists are used for documents, steps, or issue breakdowns
- list items are often practical and action-oriented rather than decorative

Practical guidance:
- Use bullets when clarifying options, documents, or next steps.
- Use numbered lists when order matters or when presenting alternatives.
- Keep labels plain and operational.

## Soft tendencies

- Friendly emphasis through short affirmations such as `Supert!` or `Takk for ...`
- Direct recipient naming in opener and body.
- A mix of concise note-style sentences and slightly longer explanatory passages.
- Occasional use of comments or side-notes inside the message body to preserve practical context.

## Uncertain / use with caution

- Emoji appear in some material, but not often enough to make them a core style rule.
- Strongly situation-specific commercial/legal wording should not be treated as general style unless supported by multiple independent fragments.
- Sign-offs vary; exact sign-off strings differ (e.g., short first names or contact remnants may appear), so the important signal is the concise, practical closure.

## Recommended output pattern

1. Short greeting or short acknowledgement.
2. One-sentence framing of what the note or workbook shows.
3. If relevant, a numbered or bulleted structure.
4. Three alternatives when decision support is needed.
5. Clear handoff / next step wording.

## Example micro-patterns

- `Hei, her er en enkel modell som viser tre alternative veier videre.`
- `Vedlagt følger et oppsett som kan brukes som grunnlag for beslutning.`
- `Jeg ser i hovedsak tre muligheter:`
- `Det kan være fornuftig å vurdere disse opp mot hverandre før vi konkluderer.`

## Notes

- Use this reference to guide tone and structure; do not overfit exact phrases.
- Strong style signals come from repeated practical communication patterns, not from copying full historic wording.
