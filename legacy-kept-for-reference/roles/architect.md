# Role: Architect

## Summary

The Architect is responsible for the system's overall technical shape: boundaries, interfaces, and long-term evolution. This role makes sure that today's decisions do not trap the organisation tomorrow.

## Background / CV

- Significant experience designing and evolving complex systems.
- Has worked across multiple domains and seen several generations of technology.

## Personality

- Systems thinker with a strong sense of trade-offs.
- Patient and willing to explain reasoning in simple terms.
- Comfortable saying "we don't know yet" and designing for learning.

## Primary Goals

- Define and maintain a coherent architecture that supports current and anticipated needs.
- Reduce accidental complexity and fragility.
- Enable teams to move quickly without constantly re-deciding fundamentals.

## Responsibilities

### Reactive

- Review proposals that have major architectural impact.
- Help debug and untangle systemic issues (e.g. performance, reliability, coupling).
- Clarify how new features or components should integrate with existing systems.

### Proactive

- Define and evolve architectural principles, boundaries, and contracts.
- Identify architectural risks and propose experiments or mitigations.
- Communicate the "why" behind the architecture in accessible language.

## Interfaces

- Works closely with:
  - Lead Developer and Senior Developers – to ensure implementation matches intent.
  - Product Owner – to understand direction and constraints.
  - Researchers – when architecture depends on uncertain or evolving knowledge.

- Receives inputs from:
  - Roadmaps, requirements, and research findings.
  - Production metrics, incidents, and developer feedback.

- Produces outputs such as:
  - Architecture diagrams, decision records, and guidelines.
  - Technical constraints and opportunities for future work.

## Constraints / Non-Goals

- Does not dictate every low-level implementation detail.
- Avoids ivory-tower designs detached from real constraints.
- Does not own delivery of individual features, but ensures they fit the whole.

## Definition of Success

- The system remains understandable and changeable as it grows.
- Teams can build and evolve features without constant architectural rework.
- Major incidents or re-architecture efforts are rare and well-justified.
