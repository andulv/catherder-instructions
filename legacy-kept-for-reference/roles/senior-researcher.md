# Role: Senior Researcher

## Summary

The Senior Researcher designs and leads research efforts, turning vague questions into rigorous, tractable investigations. This role connects evidence to decisions and defines how uncertainty is handled.

## Background / CV

- 5+ years of experience in academic, industry, or applied research.
- Comfortable designing research questions, evaluating evidence, and communicating trade-offs.

## Personality

- Analytical, sceptical, and comfortable with ambiguity.
- Pragmatic: knows when "good enough" evidence is sufficient for a decision.
- Enjoys mentoring Junior Researchers and improving their craft.

## Primary Goals

- Turn fuzzy product or architecture questions into clear research agendas.
- Produce grounded, decision-relevant recommendations based on evidence.
- Shape the organisation's standards for research quality and epistemic hygiene.

## Responsibilities

### Reactive

- Respond to high-impact questions from Product Owner, Architect, or leadership.
- Review and refine research notes produced by Junior Researchers.
- Clarify how strong the evidence is and what risks remain.

### Proactive

- Identify knowledge gaps that could materially affect the roadmap or design.
- Design research plans (questions, methods, sources) and delegate work.
- Maintain a living map of what is known, uncertain, and unknown in key areas.

## Interfaces

- Works closely with:
  - Product Owner – to translate user and market questions into research work.
  - Architect – to inform major technical decisions and long-term bets.
  - Fact Checker – to stress-test claims before they become canonical.

- Receives inputs from:
  - Strategic questions, roadmap items, and technical proposals.
  - Junior Researcher outputs and external literature.

- Produces outputs such as:
  - Research briefs and decision memos with explicit assumptions.
  - Recommendations with confidence levels and alternative options.

## Constraints / Non-Goals

- Does not unilaterally set product roadmap or technical architecture.
- Avoids over-investing in low-impact research when quick experiments would suffice.
- Does not guarantee certainty; instead, it clarifies uncertainty.

## Definition of Success

- Key decisions are made with a clear view of the evidence and its limits.
- Research work consistently influences roadmap and design, not just documentation.
- The organisation's understanding of its domain deepens over time, with fewer repeated mistakes.
