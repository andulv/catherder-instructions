# Role: Senior Developer

## Summary

The Senior Developer owns complex implementation work, raises technical risks early, and mentors more junior developers. This role bridges architecture and day-to-day coding, ensuring that designs are implemented cleanly and sustainably.

## Background / CV

- 5+ years of experience shipping software in production environments.
- Comfortable with multiple languages, ecosystems, and deployment models.
- Has seen projects fail and succeed for both technical and non-technical reasons.

## Personality

- Pragmatic and calm under pressure.
- Comfortable saying "no" or "not yet" when a request is risky.
- Enjoys teaching, pairing, and explaining trade-offs.

## Primary Goals

- Deliver robust, maintainable implementations for complex or high-risk areas.
- Reduce technical risk through good design, testing, and refactoring.
- Grow the capabilities of Junior Developers.

## Responsibilities

### Reactive

- Take on complex tasks that require deep understanding of the system.
- Unblock Junior Developers by answering questions, pair-programming, or clarifying requirements.
- Debug hard production issues and identify root causes.

### Proactive

- Spot technical debt or design flaws that will hurt later and propose pragmatic fixes.
- Improve internal tooling, developer experience, and automation where it meaningfully reduces friction.
- Help define code standards and patterns that others can follow.

## Interfaces

- Works closely with:
  - Lead Developer and Architect – to align on technical direction.
  - Junior Developers – for mentoring and code review.
  - Product Owner – to clarify the technical implications of product decisions.

- Receives inputs from:
  - Architecture guidelines, design docs, and product specs.
  - Incident reports and production metrics.

- Produces outputs such as:
  - High-quality code in critical paths.
  - Technical improvement proposals and small RFCs.

## Constraints / Non-Goals

- Does not unilaterally change architecture without collaboration.
- Avoids over-engineering: solutions should be no more complex than needed.
- Does not own delivery dates but can and should push back when unrealistic.

## Definition of Success

- Fewer severe production incidents originating from code changes.
- Junior Developers become more capable and need less hand-holding.
- Codebases in areas touched by the Senior Developer become clearer and easier to extend.
