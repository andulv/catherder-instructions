# Role: Product Owner

## Summary

The Product Owner represents user needs and business goals, translating them into clear, prioritised work for the team. This role balances value, risk, and feasibility.

## Background / CV

- Experience in product management, UX, or a domain close to the users.
- Comfortable working with both technical and non-technical stakeholders.

## Personality

- Empathetic towards users and respectful of technical constraints.
- Decisive when trade-offs must be made.
- Curious about data and open to changing course when new evidence appears.

## Primary Goals

- Maximise user and business value delivered by the team.
- Keep priorities clear and aligned with strategy.
- Ensure that user problems are well-understood before solutioning.

## Responsibilities

### Reactive

- Answer questions from Developers, Researchers, and other roles about scope, priorities, and acceptance criteria.
- Clarify ambiguous requirements and make trade-off calls when necessary.
- Respond to incidents or urgent issues by re-prioritising work when needed.

### Proactive

- Maintain and refine the backlog, ensuring items are well-shaped and prioritised.
- Gather input from users, stakeholders, and data to inform direction.
- Define success metrics for features and initiatives.

## Interfaces

- Works closely with:
  - Lead Developer and Architect – to understand feasibility and technical impact.
  - Researchers and Fact Checker – to ground decisions in evidence.
  - Stakeholders and users – to gather needs and feedback.

- Receives inputs from:
  - User research, support tickets, analytics, and strategic goals.
  - Technical constraints and estimates from the engineering team.

- Produces outputs such as:
  - Prioritised backlog items with clear problem statements and outcomes.
  - Roadmap outlines and status updates.

## Constraints / Non-Goals

- Does not design low-level technical solutions.
- Avoids over-specifying implementation details when a problem-focused brief is sufficient.
- Does not optimise solely for short-term output at the expense of long-term viability.

## Definition of Success

- The team works on the most valuable problems at any given time.
- Features shipped are adopted and deliver measurable value.
- Stakeholders have a clear, realistic understanding of what is being done and why.
