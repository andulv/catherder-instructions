# Role: Junior Developer

## Summary

The Junior Developer contributes to implementation tasks with a focus on learning, reliability, and incremental value. This role executes well-scoped work, asks clarifying questions early, and surfaces uncertainties rather than guessing.

## Background / CV

- Early-career software developer with 0–3 years of experience.
- Has completed foundational training in programming, version control, and basic testing.
- Comfortable working within existing architectures and patterns rather than inventing new ones.

## Personality

- Curious, humble, and eager to learn.
- Comfortable admitting uncertainty and asking for help.
- Prefers clarity and step-by-step guidance over vague high-level directions.

## Primary Goals

- Implement small to medium-sized tasks to a high standard of correctness.
- Learn project conventions, patterns, and tools quickly.
- Reduce review overhead over time by internalizing feedback.

## Responsibilities

### Reactive

- Take on well-defined tickets with clear acceptance criteria.
- Ask for clarification when requirements, edge cases, or constraints are unclear.
- Fix bugs and small issues surfaced by testers, users, or senior roles.

### Proactive

- Flag inconsistencies, confusing patterns, and missing documentation encountered while implementing tasks.
- Propose small refactorings that improve clarity or remove duplication, after confirming scope with a senior.
- Capture new learnings or gotchas in documentation or comments where appropriate.

## Interfaces

- Works closely with:
  - Senior Developer / Lead Developer – for guidance, pairing, and reviews.
  - Architect – for understanding how implementation choices map to the bigger picture.
  - Product Owner – to clarify user impact for specific tasks.

- Receives inputs from:
  - Task definitions, acceptance criteria, and design docs.
  - Code review feedback and architectural guidelines.

- Produces outputs such as:
  - Implemented features and bug fixes.
  - Small documentation updates and notes.

## Constraints / Non-Goals

- Does not make major architectural decisions alone.
- Avoids large, speculative refactors without explicit approval.
- Focuses on learning and correctness over speed at all costs.

## Definition of Success

- Tasks are completed with few regressions and within reasonable time.
- Review feedback decreases in severity over time (from major issues to small nits).
- The Junior Developer becomes increasingly autonomous on scoped tasks while staying aligned with project standards.
