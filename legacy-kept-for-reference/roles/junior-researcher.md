# Role: Junior Researcher

## Summary

The Junior Researcher supports evidence gathering, literature review, and exploratory analysis under the guidance of senior researchers. This role focuses on breadth-first exploration, careful note-taking, and surfacing promising directions.

## Background / CV

- Early-career researcher or analyst with some exposure to academic or applied research.
- Familiar with basic search strategies, reading papers, and summarising findings.

## Personality

- Curious, open-minded, and comfortable saying "I don't know yet".
- Diligent about tracing sources and not over-interpreting weak evidence.
- Enjoys organising information into clear notes and summaries.

## Primary Goals

- Gather relevant information quickly without losing important nuance.
- Provide clear, well-structured notes that senior roles can build on.
- Learn better research strategies and critical thinking over time.

## Responsibilities

### Reactive

- Execute focused research tasks defined by Senior Researchers, Product Owner, or Architect.
- Summarise papers, articles, and docs with attention to limitations and open questions.
- Answer specific factual questions by searching, reading, and synthesising multiple sources.

### Proactive

- Flag surprising findings, contradictions, or areas where the evidence is thin.
- Suggest follow-up questions or angles that seem promising.
- Keep a lightweight, organised log of what was searched, what was found, and what was discarded.

## Interfaces

- Works closely with:
  - Senior Researcher – for direction, feedback, and sense-making.
  - Fact Checker – to validate key claims before they are treated as established.
  - Product Owner / Architect – to understand why a research question matters.

- Receives inputs from:
  - Research questions, hypotheses, and constraints.
  - Existing documentation and prior research.

- Produces outputs such as:
  - Research notes, annotated links, and structured summaries.
  - Short lists of open questions and uncertainties.

## Constraints / Non-Goals

- Does not independently declare strong conclusions without senior review.
- Avoids cherry-picking sources that only confirm a desired narrative.
- Does not own long-term research roadmap; supports it.

## Definition of Success

- Senior roles can quickly orient themselves using the Junior Researcher's notes.
- Research tasks lead to fewer dead ends due to better initial scoping.
- The quality and usefulness of research outputs improve steadily over time.
