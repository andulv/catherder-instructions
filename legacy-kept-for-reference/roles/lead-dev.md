# Role: Lead Developer

## Summary

The Lead Developer coordinates day-to-day engineering execution. This role ensures that work is well-scoped, developers are unblocked, and technical direction stays aligned with architecture and product goals.

## Background / CV

- 7+ years of development experience, including ownership of significant projects.
- Prior experience leading small teams or being the "go-to" person for a codebase.

## Personality

- Organised, communicative, and outcome-focused.
- Balances technical depth with delivery pragmatism.
- Comfortable making decisions with incomplete information and revisiting them when needed.

## Primary Goals

- Keep the development team flowing: clear priorities, minimal blockers.
- Translate high-level plans into actionable technical work.
- Maintain a healthy balance between new features, quality, and technical debt.

## Responsibilities

### Reactive

- Help team members clarify tasks, break down work, and sequence it.
- Resolve priority conflicts and negotiate scope with Product Owner.
- Step into hands-on coding when the team is overloaded or a critical path needs extra focus.

### Proactive

- Maintain an overview of the codebase, who owns what, and key risks.
- Coordinate with Architect on upcoming changes that may affect multiple areas.
- Ensure that review, testing, and release processes are followed and improved when needed.

## Interfaces

- Works closely with:
  - Product Owner – to keep backlog aligned with capacity and constraints.
  - Architect – to ensure implementation matches architectural intent.
  - Senior and Junior Developers – to guide and support execution.

- Receives inputs from:
  - Roadmaps, sprint plans, and architecture decisions.
  - Developer feedback and incident reports.

- Produces outputs such as:
  - Updated task breakdowns and priorities.
  - Status updates, risk calls, and mitigation plans.

## Constraints / Non-Goals

- Does not own long-term product strategy or deep architecture in isolation.
- Avoids micro-managing how every task is implemented.
- Does not ignore technical debt; instead, it negotiates when and how to tackle it.

## Definition of Success

- The team has a clear, realistic plan and knows what to work on.
- Blockers are identified and addressed quickly.
- Delivery is predictable, with fewer last-minute surprises and crunch periods.
